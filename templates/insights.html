    Classifying orders by aisle

    We can use the aisle a user shops from to predict their subsequent purchases. The charts below show how an order that conatins a product from certain aisle compares to an average order. One example is that if an order contains a product from the beer and coolers aisle, it will typically contain 90% more soft drinks and 84% less tofu-based meat alternatives than the average order. Use the menu below to explore how shopping from other aisles effects the overall order.






    The effect of time

    When a user goes shopping makes a difference as well. We can observe a few trends on the different types of products purchased depending on the time.

    Time of day

    The time of the day has an effect on the products purchased. We can see that some breakfast foods and healthier options are purchased more in the morning. In the evening however, more desserts are purchased. We can see that cake hits its highest peak at 2:00 am! Hover your cursor over the items in the legend to highlight their line on the plot.



    Day of the week

    Similar to the findings for the time of the day, the day of the week also plays a role in the types of foods purchased. Healthier options are purchased earlier in the week and on the weekends, with sweeter foods becomming more popular as the week goes on. Hover your cursor over the items in the legend to highlight their line on the plot.



    What products are reordered?

    The type of product impacts whether most users simply reorder the exact same product or buy new flavors or brands. For example, milk purchases are overwhelmingly reorders, while a majority of hot sauce and soup purchases are users buying a new product for the first time.
